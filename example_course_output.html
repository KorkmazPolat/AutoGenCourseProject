What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. The term may also be applied to any machine that exhibits traits associated with a human mind such as learning and problem-solving.

1. Categories of AI

AI is generally categorized into two types:

  • Narrow AI (Weak AI): Designed to perform a narrow task (e.g., facial recognition). This is the only form of AI that exists today.
  • General AI (Strong AI): A system with generalized human cognitive abilities. This is currently theoretical.

2. A Brief History

The field of AI research was founded as an academic discipline in 1956. Key milestones include:

  • 1950: Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test.
  • 1997: IBM's Deep Blue beats Garry Kasparov at chess.
  • 2012: Deep Learning revolution begins with AlexNet.
  • 2023: Large Language Models transform the industry.

3. Applications

AI is transforming industries globally:

  • Healthcare: Diagnosis and personalized medicine.
  • Finance: Fraud detection and trading.
  • Transportation: Autonomous vehicles.

Fundamentals of Machine Learning

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that focuses on building systems that learn from data. Instead of explicitly programming rules, we feed data to algorithms that build their own logic.

Types of Machine Learning

1. Supervised Learning

The model is trained on labeled data. It learns a mapping from inputs (x) to outputs (y).

  • Examples: Spam classification, House price prediction.

2. Unsupervised Learning

The model is trained on unlabeled data. It finds hidden patterns or structures in the input data.

  • Examples: Customer segmentation, Anomaly detection.

3. Reinforcement Learning

An agent learns to make decisions by performing actions in an environment and receiving rewards or penalties.
